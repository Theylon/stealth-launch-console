# Market Context Model

Use this model to normalize raw company inputs into a reusable brief.

## Required fields

- `Company`: What the company sells and the stage it appears to be in.
- `Product`: The concrete product, not the mission statement.
- `ICP hypotheses`: The segments currently in play, not just the stated target.
- `Buyer or user`: Who feels the pain, who signs, and who blocks.
- `Core job`: What the customer is trying to get done.
- `Alternatives`: What they do today if they do not buy this product.
- `Proof`: Measurable outcomes, customer evidence, unique capabilities, or credible signals.
- `Pricing and GTM motion`: Self-serve, sales-led, PLG, annual contract, pilot, usage-based, and similar constraints.
- `Goals`: The business objective for the copy or strategy work.
- `Tone and constraints`: Words to avoid, brand posture, legal limits, or market promises that cannot be made.

## Normalization rules

- Use customer-language when available; use founder-language only when no customer signal exists.
- Separate segment facts from segment aspirations.
- Write alternatives broadly enough to include status quo, manual work, and adjacent competitors.
- Treat proof as a gating item. Weak proof should stay visible instead of getting polished away.

## Source synthesis

- *Obviously Awesome*: Anchor the brief around real alternatives, best-fit customers, and differentiated value.
- *Crossing the Chasm*: Preserve segment differences instead of collapsing them into one broad market.
- *Competing Against Luck* and *Value Proposition Design*: Capture the job, pains, and desired progress underneath feature requests.
- *StoryBrand*: Keep the customer problem legible and concrete.
