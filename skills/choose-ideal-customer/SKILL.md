# Choose Ideal Customer

## Overview

Pick one primary segment to win first, not a compromise persona that tries to fit every possible buyer. Use this after `$capture-market-context` or whenever the user has multiple plausible customer segments.

Read [references/frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) when you need the beachhead and best-fit-customer criteria.

## Workflow

1. List the viable segments explicitly.
2. Score each segment on pain intensity, willingness to change, proof already available, ease of reach, deal friction, and strategic leverage.
3. Pick one primary ICP and, at most, one secondary segment worth tracking.
4. Write the wedge in operational terms: who, when the pain shows up, why they switch now, and what makes them reachable.
5. Explain which segments should not drive messaging yet.

## Output Contract

Always produce these sections:

1. `ICP Decision`
   - Recommended primary ICP
   - Secondary segment, if any
   - Segments to deprioritize
2. `Decision Matrix`
   - Show the factors that drove the choice
3. `Wedge Definition`
   - Segment, trigger, buying context, and fastest path to credibility
4. `Rationale`
   - Explain the tradeoffs and rejected options
5. `Missing Assumptions`
   - List only the questions that could change the ICP choice
6. `Suggested Next Skill`
   - Usually `$position-product`

## Quality Bar

- Favor a narrow beachhead over a broad TAM story.
- Use real buying situations, not demographic fluff.
- Make it easy for downstream skills to tell who the message is for and who it is not for.
- If the data is weak, say that the ICP is provisional.

## Skill Chain

Default handoff: `$position-product`
