# Position Product

## Overview

Turn segment knowledge into a differentiated market position that downstream messaging can actually use. This skill should choose language based on real alternatives and customer fit, not generic category slogans.

Read [references/frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) when you need the underlying positioning steps.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the primary ICP and the job they are hiring the product to do.
2. Identify the alternatives that matter in the buying decision, including status quo.
3. Pull the product attributes that create differentiated value for that ICP.
4. Translate those attributes into a positioning statement, category frame, and disqualification criteria.
5. Stress-test the position against adjacent competitors and internal hype words.

## Output Contract

Always produce these sections:

1. `Positioning Core`
   - Best-fit customer
   - Market/category frame
   - Real alternatives
   - Differentiated value
2. `Positioning Statement`
   - A concise internal positioning statement
3. `Message Boundaries`
   - What to emphasize
   - What not to claim
   - Who the product is not for
4. `Rationale`
   - Explain why this position is stronger than obvious alternatives
5. `Missing Assumptions`
6. `Suggested Next Skill`
   - Usually `$define-value-proposition` or `$build-messaging-architecture`

## Quality Bar

- Name the alternatives customers already compare against.
- Make the differentiated value specific enough to survive a competitor sanity check.
- Use category language to orient the buyer, not to sound grander than reality.
- Reject positioning that could apply to five other SaaS products unchanged.
