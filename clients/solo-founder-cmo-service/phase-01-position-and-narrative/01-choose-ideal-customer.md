# Approved Deliverable

## ICP Decision

### Recommended Primary ICP

Solo founders with:

- a live product or service
- at least some customer or launch signal
- no senior marketing leader
- a founder-dependent narrative that has not yet been turned into a system

### Secondary Segment

Solo founders with early traction who now need conversion, pricing, and growth-ops improvements rather than only foundational positioning.

### Deprioritized Segments

- idea-stage founders
- teams with mature in-house marketing leadership
- businesses that mainly need paid-media execution at scale

## Decision Matrix

- pain intensity: high
- urgency to switch: high once traction starts exposing messaging gaps
- proof availability: moderate and growing
- reachability: high through founder channels and direct positioning
- complexity: manageable because the buyer is also the operator

## Wedge Definition

The wedge is the founder who has already built something real, knows the product deeply, but still cannot explain it crisply or turn that understanding into a repeatable marketing engine.

## Rationale

This ICP is narrow enough to sell to clearly and broad enough to cover both B2B and B2C variants under one service. It also matches the strongest current proof, since the service itself is being built in exactly this stage.

## Missing Assumptions

- whether the first marketed wedge should bias more strongly toward software founders
- whether B2C consumer-product founders need a cheaper or lighter entry package
