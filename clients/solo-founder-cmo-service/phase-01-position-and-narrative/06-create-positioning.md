# Approved Deliverable

## Positioning Canvas

### Category Frame

- primary frame: fractional CMO system for solo founders
- category move: carve out a narrower, founder-specific operating model rather than invent a totally new category
- buying context: founders are already comparing agencies, freelancers, AI tools, and early executive hires

### Best-Fit Customer

- post-product, pre-scale solo founders
- founders with a live offer and at least some market signal
- founders who are still the only reliable source of product and customer narrative
- founders without a senior in-house marketing leader

### Secondary Segments

- B2B solo founders with a complex offer, longer buyer journey, and proof-heavy buying motion
- B2C solo founders with a clearer product but weak offer articulation, landing-page conversion, or retention loops
- founders preparing for a meaningful launch, reposition, or pricing shift

### Deprioritized Segments

- idea-stage founders
- venture-backed teams already staffed with strong PMM or growth leadership
- companies seeking heavy paid-media buying or large-volume creative production

## Market Alternatives

### Direct Alternatives

- generic fractional CMO consultants
- boutique agencies offering strategy retainers
- senior freelancers covering messaging or launch work

### Indirect Alternatives

- founder-led do-it-yourself marketing
- patchwork contractor stacks
- premature full-time CMO hiring
- disconnected AI prompting without process, review, or file discipline

### Status Quo Problem

Founders do not lack effort. They lack a coherent system that turns product knowledge into a stable narrative and then keeps later assets aligned to it.

## Differentiation Matrix

| Dimension | Solo Founder CMO System | Agency Retainer | Freelancer Stack | Full-Time CMO | Generic AI Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| narrative continuity | high | variable | low | medium to high | low |
| output scope | strategy through monetization | usually channel- or campaign-bound | narrow | broad but expensive | fragmented |
| founder affordability | medium | medium to high | medium | low | high |
| artifact retention | high | variable | medium | medium | low |
| review discipline | explicit | variable | variable | implicit | none |
| speed to first strategic clarity | high | medium | low | low | medium |

## Differentiators That Matter

### 1. One Narrative Spine

Positioning, website copy, launch assets, and monetization decisions are built from one approved message architecture instead of separate briefs.

### 2. System, Not Advice Only

The service does not stop at strategy recommendations. It turns decisions into reusable deliverables, saved artifacts, and founder-facing proof.

### 3. Founder-Appropriate Scope

The service is deliberately shaped for the stage where a founder needs senior leverage but not a full department.

### 4. Proof Through Dogfooding

The offer itself is being built with the same sequence it sells, which gives it immediate internal proof and reusable sales material.

## Reasons to Believe

- a mapped lifecycle from intake through monetization
- outcome-based package ladder instead of a vague retainer
- client workspace structure with approved deliverables and proof narratives
- existing dogfood artifacts showing the system applied to itself

## Positioning Statement

For post-product, pre-scale solo founders who have built something real but still lack a market-ready story, Solo Founder CMO System is a fractional CMO system that turns founder knowledge into positioning, messaging, website assets, launch systems, and repeatable growth decisions. Unlike agencies, freelancer stacks, or disconnected AI tools, it keeps strategy and execution tied to one documented narrative spine.

## Strategic Implications For The Website

- lead with the category frame immediately
- show the founder stage clearly: live product, some signal, weak narrative system
- make the narrative-spine advantage concrete, not abstract
- use proof artifacts and workflow structure as credibility, not only claims
- sell entry through packages, not through a generic monthly retainer pitch

## Risks And Watchouts

- the phrase `fractional CMO system` is strong internally but may still need market testing against `CMO as a service`
- the site should not over-index on the AI workflow mechanics at the expense of founder outcomes
- category language should stay crisp and avoid invented-category fluff

## Missing Assumptions

- whether software founders should be the first narrower acquisition wedge
- which package should be the primary hero CTA once the sales flow is live
