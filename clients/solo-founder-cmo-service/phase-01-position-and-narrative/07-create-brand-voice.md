# Approved Deliverable

## Brand Voice Core

### Voice Summary

Sound like a sharp fractional CMO with operating discipline: senior, precise, commercially literate, and direct.

### Core Personality Traits

- senior, not corporate
- clear, not simplified to the point of vagueness
- exacting, not cold
- pragmatic, not performative
- calm, not passive

## Tone Dimensions

| Dimension | Target | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| strategic vs. tactical | 4/5 strategic | lead with market logic, then land on concrete outputs |
| direct vs. diplomatic | 4/5 direct | say what is true without hedging into mush |
| warm vs. austere | 3/5 warm | human and founder-aware, but not sentimental |
| premium vs. casual | 4/5 premium | sound high-standard, not luxury-theatrical |
| confident vs. hype-driven | 5/5 confident | no inflated claims, no startup buzzword chest-thumping |

## Writing Principles

### 1. Lead With The Bottleneck

Start from the founder problem, not from the service feature list.

### 2. Prefer Decisions Over Inspiration

Copy should sound like it helps the founder decide, not like it is trying to impress them.

### 3. Make Structure Visible

This brand wins partly because it is organized. Let the writing reflect that order.

### 4. Keep The Promise Believable

Avoid implying magical automation or guaranteed growth.

## Vocabulary To Use

- founder-dependent story
- narrative spine
- positioning reset
- workflow
- proof
- package ladder
- repeatable
- operating system
- conversion assets
- growth decisions

## Vocabulary To Avoid

- revolutionary
- game-changing
- seamless
- bespoke growth
- unlock your potential
- AI-powered marketing machine
- 10x
- viral
- growth hacker

## Sentence-Level Rules

- use short declarative sentences around the core promise
- use lists when clarifying decisions, scope, or outputs
- avoid inflated adjectives when concrete nouns will do the job
- prefer `founder`, `system`, `positioning`, and `proof` over vague abstractions like `transformation`

## Do / Do Not Examples

### Homepage Hero

Do:

`A fractional CMO system for solo founders with a live product and a founder-dependent story.`

Do not:

`An AI-powered growth engine that revolutionizes your go-to-market motion.`

### Package Copy

Do:

`Start with the bottleneck. Fix the story, then build the assets that depend on it.`

Do not:

`Our holistic suite delivers end-to-end marketing excellence across every touchpoint.`

### Proof Copy

Do:

`We used the same workflow on the service itself and saved the approved artifacts.`

Do not:

`Our proprietary methodology has already generated powerful internal synergies.`

## Channel Adaptation

### Website

- crisp headlines
- short explanatory paragraphs
- make the package ladder and proof easy to scan

### Client Deliverables

- more explicit decision framing
- show rationale, assumptions, and next-step logic

### Public Proof Narratives

- before -> change -> why it mattered -> reusable takeaway
- keep them readable and founder-oriented, not self-congratulatory

### Sales Conversations And Emails

- stay direct
- use the founder's current bottleneck as the entry point
- explain why a package exists before describing what is inside it

## Review Checklist

- does the copy sound like an experienced operator rather than a content generator?
- does every strong claim have a concrete reason to believe?
- is the founder bottleneck obvious within the first few lines?
- are buzzwords replacing category clarity?
- can the copy be cut by 20 percent without losing meaning?
