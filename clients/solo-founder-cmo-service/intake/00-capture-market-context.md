# Approved Deliverable

## Market Context Brief

### Company and Product

The business is a productized `CMO as a Service` offer built on a reusable marketing skill library. The product is not software sold directly to customers; it is a fractional CMO system that turns founder context into positioning, messaging, website assets, launch systems, and monetization improvements.

### Target Customer and Buyer

- primary buyer: solo founder
- stage: post-product, pre-scale
- market types: B2B and B2C
- buying trigger: the founder has traction or signal, but marketing is still fragmented, founder-dependent, or inconsistent

### Core Job or Problem

The founder needs senior marketing structure without hiring a full team. They need one system that can clarify the story, ship the right assets, and keep decisions connected.

### Current Alternatives

- founder does all marketing alone
- hire a generic freelancer or agency
- hire a full-time CMO too early
- use disconnected AI prompts and templates
- stitch together specialists without one narrative owner

### Value Claims and Proof

Value claims:

- strategy and execution stay connected
- every deliverable sits on one narrative spine
- the service can move from positioning to website to launch to monetization
- the system is reusable and document-first

Existing proof:

- 34 specialized marketing skills already exist in the library
- the service is being dogfooded on itself
- the workflow now includes client workspaces, proof artifacts, and phase sequencing

### Pricing and GTM Motion

- pricing model: outcome packages first, ongoing layer second
- GTM motion: founder-led sales through positioning, website, package docs, and proof artifacts
- commercial assets still missing: pricing sheet, website, onboarding questionnaire

### Tone and Constraints

- tone: senior, practical, direct, non-fluffy
- avoid: agency vagueness, generic AI-tool language, hype-first positioning
- maintain: one core offer with B2B and B2C branches

## Known Unknowns

- exact public pricing
- strongest first founder-proof story
- which package should be the main entry offer
- how much the B2C branch needs dedicated examples

## Rationale

The intake compresses the repo into one usable market brief so downstream skill runs do not need to rediscover the same context. It also makes the main strategic constraint explicit: this is a service system, not a generic marketing library.
